Mahomes bought 8 acres of vacant land in Loch Lloyd — a gated community 20 miles south of Arrowhead Stadium — for $400,000 in 2020 and spent three years building his dream compound from scratch. He and Brittany finally moved in during 2023.

The estate is less a house than an athlete's private paradise. A 50-yard football field with "Mahomes" painted in the end zone takes center stage. There's a par-three golf hole with a bunker and putting green, a custom pool with swim-up bar and water slide, a private fishing pond, a state-of-the-art gym, a movie theater, and a wine room. In October 2024, the compound was burglarized while Mahomes was traveling — prompting a significant security overhaul.

LeBron paid $36.75M in 2020 for a historic Beverly Hills hilltop estate — a 1930s Mediterranean mansion that had been owned by soap opera legends Bill and Lee Phillip Bell, and before them was leased by Howard Hughes to Katharine Hepburn.

In a move that stunned even Beverly Hills insiders, LeBron demolished the entire original mansion in 2023 and is building two brand-new custom homes on the 2.72-acre lot. The primary mansion will be approximately 16,000 sq ft; a second home will be 6,550 sq ft. When complete, the compound is expected to be worth over $100M — nearly 3× what he paid for the land. Planned features include an infinity pool, outdoor kitchen, basement theater, spa, solar panels, and a private guard house.

Curry lives in Atherton — zip code 94027, consistently ranked the most expensive in the United States, home to tech billionaires, Stanford professors, and venture capital royalty. His estate sits in the Lloyden Park neighborhood on 1.7 acres.

The 17,700 sq ft modern Mediterranean home was purchased quietly in December 2020 through an LLC for approximately $30M. Curry had previously owned and sold a smaller Atherton home for $31.15M — making him one of the few athletes who has transacted twice in the $30M+ range in the same zip code. The Bay Area real estate market means his properties are surrounded by some of the most valuable land in the world.

In a move that defines his personality, Josh Allen bought 31 acres of land next to his existing Orchard Park home for just $500,000 in 2021 — then built a custom compound from scratch. For a quarterback earning $43M per year, it's a shockingly practical and rooted choice.

The estate is 10 minutes from Highmark Stadium and built for both athletic performance and family life. Features include an infinity pool, private pond, outdoor kitchen, tennis court, a manicured golf hole, a golf simulator, a home theater, and a full gym. The basement entertainment suite has a full bar. Allen married actress Hailee Steinfeld in 2025 and subsequently delisted his California beach house — signaling his long-term commitment to Buffalo.

Crosby bought the Sewickley land for $800,000 in 2011 — the same year he sold his previous Pittsburgh farmhouse to Penguins president David Morehouse — and built his current custom Colonial-style estate from scratch.

At 10,642 sq ft, it's a large but understated home for one of hockey's greatest players. Crosby is famously private — he kept the same Sewickley address for over a decade, rarely photographed outside it. Three fireplaces, built-in bookshelves, an elegant stone walkway, and a finished basement. The property sits in Sewickley's most exclusive enclave, about 12 miles from PPG Paints Arena.
