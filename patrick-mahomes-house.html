The Spyglass compound

When Patrick Mahomes bought 8 acres of vacant land in Loch Lloyd — a guard-gated community in Belton, Missouri, 20 miles south of Arrowhead Stadium — for $400,000 in September 2020, nobody knew exactly what he was building. Three years later, the answer became clear: the most personalized athletic compound in the NFL.

Mahomes and his wife Brittany spent three years constructing their dream home from scratch, moving in during 2023 after their third child was born. The address, 16001 Spyglass Ct, sits in a resort-style gated village where privacy is the primary amenity. The land cost $400,000. The finished compound is now estimated to be worth over $8 million.

In a 2023 Netflix documentary, Mahomes revealed this was exactly the house he had always wanted. Every feature was chosen deliberately — not for show, but for the rhythms of his life as an athlete, a husband, and a father of three.

50 yards
A private football field — with "Mahomes" painted in the end zone — sits on the estate grounds. It serves as both a play area for his children and a private training facility during the NFL offseason. Andy Reid reportedly approved the idea immediately when Mahomes suggested it.

The burglary — and its aftermath

Breaking · May 2026
Three Chilean nationals arrested in connection with Mahomes compound burglary

Three suspects linked to the October 2024 break-in at Mahomes' Loch Lloyd estate were arrested in Argentina in May 2026 and face extradition to the United States. The arrests followed their detention after a separate burglary at the home of Argentine tennis star Juan Martín del Potro.

The suspects are part of a South American burglary ring of seven defendants charged in a January 2025 federal complaint. Investigators allege the crew used publicly available NFL game schedules to target athletes while they were competing — approaching homes through wooded areas at night, breaking windows or prying open sliding doors with crowbars.

The ring also targeted Travis Kelce (whose home was burglarized just two days after Mahomes', on October 8, 2024 — the same week Taylor Swift was at the Chiefs game), Joe Burrow, and Bobby Portis. Total estimated losses across all targets exceeded $2 million.

The October 6, 2024 burglary at the Spyglass compound occurred just after midnight. A bodyguard reported the incident to Cass County Sheriff's Office. There was no sign of forced entry. The specific items taken from Mahomes' home were not fully disclosed, though federal documents indicate combined losses from both Mahomes' and Kelce's residences exceeded $5,000.

In the days following, Mahomes spoke to media: "Obviously it's frustrating. It's disappointing. Something that you don't want to have happen to really anybody, but obviously yourself." He subsequently made significant security upgrades to the compound, and the Loch Lloyd community itself implemented additional security measures for all residents.

Mahomes did not put the property up for sale — confirming his long-term commitment to his custom-built home.

The complete real estate journey

Mahomes has built one of the NFL's most interesting real estate portfolios — moving from a $350K rookie penthouse to a fully custom $8M compound in just six years.

Why Loch Lloyd?

Most NFL quarterbacks of Mahomes' stature gravitates toward the obvious luxury markets — Scottsdale, Miami, Los Angeles. Mahomes chose Belton, Missouri. The choice says everything about his priorities.

Loch Lloyd is a resort-style gated community in Cass County, approximately 20 miles south of Arrowhead Stadium. It's home to a golf course, tennis courts, and a private lake — but what matters most is what it isn't: it isn't public, it isn't photographed, and it isn't the kind of neighborhood where tabloid photographers lurk outside the gate.

The drive to Arrowhead takes 25-30 minutes. The drive to Brittany's family takes minutes. The space for three children to grow up with a football field and a fishing pond is built into the property itself. In a league where players increasingly treat their homes as investment vehicles and social media backdrops, Mahomes built a compound for living — not performing.
