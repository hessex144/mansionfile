Atherton's zip code has been ranked #1 most expensive in the United States for nearly a decade straight. Median home price: $7M+. Median household income: $525,000. Curry has now transacted twice in this zip code at $30M+.

Why Atherton?

When most NBA superstars imagine their dream home, they picture Beverly Hills, Malibu, or Miami. Stephen Curry chose Atherton — a quiet, gated town of 7,000 people in the heart of Silicon Valley, 35 minutes south of San Francisco and 30 minutes from Chase Center.

Atherton is where tech billionaires go when they want to disappear. No Rodeo Drive. No paparazzi hotels. No celebrity neighbors posting Instagram tours of the neighborhood. It is deliberately, aggressively private. The town has no commercial district, no hotels, and some of the strictest zoning regulations in California. Lots are large, streets are quiet, and the community is — by design — invisible to the outside world.

For Curry, who has raised four children in the Bay Area his entire career and explicitly cites "safety and privacy for us and our kids" as his top priority, it is a perfect match. He is the only athlete most people could name who lives in a neighborhood dominated by hedge fund managers, semiconductor founders, and Stanford professors.

The Lloyden Park estate

Curry and Ayesha purchased their current Atherton home in December 2020 for $30 million — nine months before selling their previous Atherton property. Located near the Lloyden Park neighborhood on a gated, flat 1.7-acre lot, the home was custom-built and features a design that blends modern and traditional California architecture.

The house spans 17,700 square feet with seven bedrooms, nine bathrooms, and all the amenities a family of six requires: a basketball court, home theater, wine cellar, multiple entertainment spaces, a pool, and full smart home integration. Floor-to-ceiling windows flood the interior with Northern California light and connect the living spaces to the surrounding gardens.

What distinguishes this home from comparable celebrity real estate isn't its price — it's its purpose. This is unambiguously a family home, not a showpiece. Curry has said explicitly that privacy and his children's safety drove the decision to stay in Atherton rather than move to more glamorous markets.

The Malibu estate — a different world

In November 2021, Curry made a second, completely different kind of real estate move: a $50 million beach estate in Malibu, California. Where the Atherton home is about family and privacy, the Malibu property is about investment and indulgence.

The estate sits on 17 acres with direct Pacific Ocean frontage. At 12,637 square feet with just four bedrooms — technically not enough rooms for all of his children — it's clear this isn't primarily a family residence. It's held as an investment property, though the Curry family uses it as a Southern California retreat.

The interior design is striking. The home features an exquisite fossil collection displayed throughout, and a custom cabinet in the living area that transforms into a fully stocked bar. Panoramic Pacific Ocean views from almost every room. Combined with the Atherton estate, Curry now holds approximately $80M in California residential real estate.

The affordable housing letter

In January 2023, Curry and Ayesha sent a letter to Atherton's mayor and city council opposing a state-mandated affordable housing development that would have been built adjacent to their property. The letter stated they had "major concerns in terms of both privacy and safety with three-story townhomes looming directly behind us."

The Currys requested the town commit to "investing in considerably taller fencing and landscaping to block sight lines onto" their property if the development proceeded. The letter attracted significant media attention given the contrast between Curry's public image and the opposition to affordable housing near his $30M estate. The state-mandated housing effort was part of California's broader push to compel local governments to permit more housing supply or lose funding.

The bigger picture

Curry's real estate portfolio tells a specific story: a player who became wealthy gradually, invested carefully, and chose roots over flash. His Bay Area concentration is unusual — most NBA players cycle through multiple markets — and it reflects his commitment to a single franchise and a single community.

Having now transacted twice at $30M+ in the same zip code, Curry has demonstrated the kind of disciplined, high-conviction real estate approach that mirrors his shooting philosophy: patient, precise, and compounding over time. Total California real estate holdings of approximately $80M from two properties, plus a career in the Bay Area that has seen consistent appreciation in one of the world's most valuable real estate markets.

He has also demonstrated something rarer: the willingness to sell and upgrade rather than hoard. The Orinda, Walnut Creek, Alamo, and West Atherton properties were all sold — often at a profit — as his family grew and his means expanded. It's a portfolio managed with the same discipline as his $62.6M Warriors contract negotiation.
