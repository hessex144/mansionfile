Ohtani purchased the estate in May 2024 — the same spring he married Mamiko Tanaka — under an LLC named "Decopin," after his Golden Retriever who went viral for high-fiving him at his 2023 AL MVP announcement. The home sits in La Cañada Flintridge, a quiet, wealthy suburb tucked into the Verdugo Mountain foothills north of Los Angeles. It's 13 miles and approximately 20 minutes from Dodger Stadium.

The three-story home features a private movie theater, basketball court (his wife Tanaka is a former professional basketball player), gym, sauna, outdoor kitchen, infinity pool, and a four-car garage. Ohtani paid $500K below the $8.35M asking price — the kind of disciplined negotiation you'd expect from a man who deferred $680M of his $700M contract. The surrounding property is filled with mature oak trees, providing natural privacy on the roughly 1-acre lot.

Fresh off signing his nine-year, $360M contract and being named the 16th captain in Yankees history, Judge purchased a penthouse at The Cortland — a brand-new 25-story luxury tower in Chelsea designed by two of architecture's most celebrated names: Robert A.M. Stern Architects and Olson Kundig.

The exact purchase price was not publicly disclosed. Two units on the 22nd floor sold for a combined $40M according to reports — whether Judge purchased one or both is unconfirmed. The 7,000 sq ft penthouse features floor-to-ceiling windows, high ceilings, and panoramic Hudson River views. Judge was reportedly "building it out" at time of purchase — customizing the space from its blank-canvas state. The building features nearly 20,000 sq ft of amenities including a 75-foot pool, spa, yoga studio, game room, and screening room that doubles as a performance space. Judge told reporters he and wife Samantha Bracksieck "came to the decision that was in our hearts, which is we belong in New York, we belong in this city."

Mike Trout is baseball's most anomalous superstar. He plays in Los Angeles — one of the world's most glamorous cities — and he lives in Millville, New Jersey: a small city of 28,000 people in Cumberland County, South Jersey, where he was born and raised. He built a custom home there during his early career and has never left.

Trout grew up a Philadelphia Phillies fan in a baseball family (his father played four years of minor league ball). He attended Millville High School, where he became a legendary local figure. His attachment to South Jersey is genuine and consistent — he co-developed Trout National – The Reserve, an 18-hole private golf course in Vineland designed by Tiger Woods, which opened in April 2026, just minutes from his home. He has said repeatedly that Millville is home and always will be.

Trout National – The Reserve is a private destination golf club built on 280 acres of former silica sand mining land in Vineland — just minutes from where Trout grew up. The 18-hole, par-72 course was designed by Tiger Woods and his TGR Design firm, with construction completing in October 2024 and the course opening for member preview play in April 2026. Trout partnered with local developer John Ruga on the project. Five-star lodging, a restaurant, and a wedding chapel are included. "Getting Tiger to design it is crazy," Trout said. "If you had told me this would happen one day, I would have said you are crazy."
