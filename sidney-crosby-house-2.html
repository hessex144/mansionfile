Hockey's greatest player has lived in the same Sewickley, Pennsylvania home since 2013. He bought the land for $800,000, built a 10,642 sq ft colonial estate from scratch, and hasn't moved since. A portrait of loyalty in an era of perpetual relocation.

Sidney Crosby bought the land for his Sewickley estate in 2011 and moved in during 2013. He has not moved since. In a league — in a culture — where superstars relocate constantly, Crosby has stayed put for over a decade, in a modest suburb of Pittsburgh, 12 miles from the rink where he plays every home game.

One city. One home. Fifteen years.

Sidney Crosby has played his entire NHL career for the Pittsburgh Penguins. He's won three Stanley Cups in Pittsburgh. He's taken repeated pay cuts — "hometown discounts" in the truest sense — to keep the team competitive. And through all of it, he has lived in the same Sewickley home he built in 2013.

Sewickley is a picturesque borough 12 miles northwest of Pittsburgh, known for its charming streets, tree-lined neighborhoods, and complete absence of celebrity culture. There are no paparazzi at the Sewickley farmers market. There is no TMZ presence outside the gate on Creek Drive. For a player who has been one of hockey's biggest global stars since he was 18 years old, that invisibility is the point.

Crosby bought the land for $800,000 in 2011 — the year he sold his previous Pittsburgh farmhouse to Penguins president David Morehouse — demolished the existing structure, and built his current home from scratch. Construction completed in 2013. He's been there ever since.

Crosby returns to Cole Harbour — the small Nova Scotia town where he grew up and first learned to skate — every offseason. The lakefront property on Grand Lake offers the same quiet and privacy as the Sewickley estate, with the added familiarity of home. For a man who has been famous since childhood, returning to where no one treats him differently is a meaningful act.

The real estate as biography

Most athletes' real estate portfolios tell the story of how famous they became. Crosby's tells the story of who he decided to remain.

He sold his first Pittsburgh home to his own team's president. He built his current home from the ground up. He's never chased a more glamorous market. He has never bought in Los Angeles, Miami, or New York. He has a lakefront property in the small Canadian town where he grew up. That's the entire portfolio.

The Crosby real estate philosophy

"Safety and privacy for us and our kids continues to be our top priority." Every property choice Crosby has made reflects this. Sewickley for the season. Cole Harbour for the summer. Nowhere else.

The full timeline

2005
Drafted #1 overall — moves to Pittsburgh
Crosby is drafted first overall by the Pittsburgh Penguins at 17 years old. He famously moves in with then-Penguins owner Mario Lemieux's family during his early years — a remarkable detail about a player who would become one of the sport's all-time greats.
2010
First owned Pittsburgh home — farmhouse
Crosby purchases a New England-style farmhouse near Pittsburgh for $2.4 million — a 6-bedroom, multi-bathroom estate on 6.58 acres in Sewickley. After one year, he sells it to Penguins CEO David Morehouse for $2.5 million — a $100K profit — to upgrade to a custom-built home.
2011
Buys land on Creek Drive, Sewickley
Pays $800,000 for the land at 214 Creek Drive, demolishes the existing structure, and begins construction on his custom-designed colonial estate. The choice of Sewickley — quiet, private, suburban — over Pittsburgh proper reflects his priorities exactly.
2013
Moves into current home — and stays
Construction of the 10,642 sq ft colonial estate completes. Crosby moves in. The home has three fireplaces, five bedrooms, built-in bookshelves, a finished basement, and a gourmet kitchen. It is renovated in 2013 to blend contemporary amenities with classic design. Crosby has not moved since — making this one of the longest-held primary residences of any active superstar athlete in North American sports.
Ongoing
Stanley Cups won from this address: 3
Crosby has won the Stanley Cup in 2009, 2016, and 2017 — all while a Pittsburgh Penguin, all while living within 12 miles of the arena. He has twice signed contract extensions below market value to keep Pittsburgh competitive. His home is a physical anchor for a career that has been defined by staying.

Sewickley — the neighborhood

Sewickley is a borough of approximately 3,500 people in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania. It sits on the Ohio River, 12 miles northwest of downtown Pittsburgh. The streets are tree-lined, the architecture is historic, and the community is — by Pittsburgh standards — very private and very wealthy.

Crosby's neighbors are not celebrities. They are surgeons, attorneys, and executives who value the same things Crosby does: access to Pittsburgh without living in Pittsburgh, privacy without gating yourself off from a real community, and a neighborhood where your children can grow up normally.

Crosby has been a visible, present member of the Sewickley community for over a decade. He's known at local restaurants. He participates in community events. He is, by all accounts, the most normal version of the world's most famous hockey player.
